    \subsection{Exponential Regression}\label{exponential-regression}

    If we believe \(y = Ca^x\) then by regressing \(x\) against \(\ln y\) we
can determine \(a\).

\[
\begin{array}{rcl}
y &=& Ca^x \\
\ln y &=& \ln C + x \ln a \\
\end{array}
\]

This is a line with slope \(\ln a\) and intercept \(\ln C\)
